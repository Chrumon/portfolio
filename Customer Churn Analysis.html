Customer Churn Analysis

During my internship with Forage in collaboration with PwC, I undertook a significant project involving a dataset provided by PhoneNow. This dataset encompassed information about PhoneNow's customers, detailing their diverse service subscriptions and the patterns of churn over time.

My primary responsibility was to generate a comprehensive report highlighting distinct customer demographics, analyzing churn trends across various services and formulating effective retention strategies.

This project not only allowed me to delve into the intricacies of customer behavior and service dynamics but also provided valuable insights that contributed to the development of strategic approaches for enhancing customer retention.

Insights Overview:
✪ The highest churn rate is observed among customers who have spent one year with the company.
✪ Monthly contract subscribers constitute a significant 88.84% of the total customer churn.
✪ Customers with a two-year contract exhibit longer tenure, whereas a considerable number of those on monthly contracts are recent additions. There is a potential risk of losing new customers, especially those on monthly subscriptions.
✪ The projected loss from churned customers amounts to $139,131.
✪ Female customers dominate the group of churned customers.
✪ The majority of churned customers do not have dependents.

Recommendations:
✪ To enhance retention, the company should incentivize new customers to commit to at least a one-year subscription.
✪ Offering discounts for customers opting for one year or longer could prove effective. Special loyalty offers should also be considered.
✪ Implementing a feedback survey for churned customers would help identify reasons for departure. This feedback system should be integrated into routine customer interactions to allow staying customers to provide ongoing evaluations of the company's services.
