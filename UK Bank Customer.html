UK Bank Customer Analysis

The goal of this analysis is to gain valuable insights and understanding about the behavior, preferences and patterns of UK bank customers.

Key Insights:
✪ The dataset includes a total of 4,014 customers, with 2,165 males and 1,849 females, distributed across 4 regions. The average age of customers is 39.
✪ The majority of customers, constituting 54%, are located in England, followed by Scotland at 28%.
✪ Approximately 78% of customers fall within the adult category.
✪ The total bank balance for the reviewed year amounts to £159,622,523.
✪ Examining the balance trend by month reveals the highest balance in September. Notably, there is a reduction in the bank balance in June following a significant increase in April. This reduction is attributed to preparations for summer.
✪ A predominant proportion of customers falls within the white-collar job classification.

Recommendations:
✪ Given that the majority of customers are from England (54%), consider tailoring marketing strategies and services to further engage this significant customer base. Additionally, explore opportunities to enhance outreach in Scotland (28%) and other regions.
✪ Recognizing the reduction in bank balance in June, attributed to summer preparations, strategize ways to mitigate this impact. Consider offering seasonal promotions or incentives to maintain customer engagement during this period.
✪ Explore opportunities to diversify services or introduce promotions that align with seasonal trends, ensuring the bank remains competitive and adaptable to changing customer behaviors.
✪ Continue monitoring balance trends by month to identify patterns and proactively address any fluctuations. Understanding the factors influencing these changes can aid in strategic decision-making.
