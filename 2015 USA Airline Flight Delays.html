2015 Performance Analysis For US Airlines

Here is my presentation of 2015 USA Airline Flight Delays created with Microsoft Power BI using the dataset provided by Quantum Analytics NG.

The dataset contains a record of 5,000,000+ commercial airline flights in 2015, compiled for the U.S. DOT Air Travel Consumer Report. Each record represents a single flight, including the airline name, flight number, origin/destination airport, flight distance, as well as scheduled/actual departure and arrival times.
The goal of this analysis is to understand the causes of flight delays in the US and provide actionable recommendations to minimize future delays

Summary of Findings:
✪ Air Traffic: The dataset captured a staggering 5,800,000+ commercial airline flights in 2015, operated by 14 airlines and servicing 322 airports across 308 cities in the United States.
✪ Monthly Flight Volume: July witnessed the highest flight volume, closely followed by August, whereas February marked the lowest flight activity.
✪ Flight Cancellations: A total of 89,000+ flights, equivalent to 1.5% of the total, were canceled. Inclement weather emerged as the primary cause, contributing to 54% of all cancellations. February experienced the highest number of cancellations, impacting the overall flight volume for the month.
✪ Delays Analysis: The analysis revealed 2,125,618 departure delays and 2,086,896 arrival delays. Southwestern Airline stood out as the most problematic, leading in departure delays, arrival delays, and flight cancellations.

Recommendation:
Consideration should be given to investing in advanced scheduling systems capable of proactively identifying potential issues, thereby preventing problems before they escalate. Additionally, fostering open communication during delays is crucial for maintaining positive customer experiences and loyalty.
