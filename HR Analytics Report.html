HR Analytics Report

In my inaugural Power BI interactive report, I utilized the HR Analytics Data provided by Quantum Analytics NG, containing comprehensive details about employees, including their name, age, job level, department, hourly rate, marital status, monthly income, years at the company and more.

The company tasked me with optimizing staff strength, prompting an analysis to determine which employees should be retained or laid off based on their service years.

Given the quantitative nature of the dataset, I introduced additional columns derived from existing ones to enhance comprehension. Notably, the "service year" column was created from the "years at the company" information and was instrumental in evaluating employees' eligibility for promotion. Employees with 10 years and above were identified as suitable for promotion.

To establish layoff criteria, "years at the company" was chosen, and employees who have spent 18 years and above were considered for layoff.

Recommendations
✪ Engage with employees identified for promotion or layoff to communicate the rationale behind these decisions and support them through the transition process.
✪ Develop a succession plan for the roles of those eligible for layoff to ensure a smooth transition and retain institutional knowledge.
✪ Implement retention strategies for high-performing employees who do not meet the criteria for promotion or layoff to maintain morale and productivity.
